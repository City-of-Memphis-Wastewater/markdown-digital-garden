``Autodesk transitioned to a subscription-based model several years ago, which changed how SKUs are handled. Instead of thousands of static part numbers for physical boxes, SKUs are now primarily categorized by **term length** (Monthly, 1-Year, 3-Year) and **offering type** (Single-user vs. Flex).

The following list reflects the primary commercial SKUs for the **2025 and 2026** release cycles.

### 1. AutoCAD (Includes Specialized Toolsets)

This is the standard "One AutoCAD" subscription. It includes the specialized toolsets for Architecture, Mechanical, Electrical, MEP, Plant 3D, Map 3D, and Raster Design.

|**Version / Term**|**SKU (Base Pattern)**|**Description**|
|---|---|---|
|**1-Year Subscription**|C1RK1-WW1243-L641|Annual subscription for 1 user|
|**3-Year Subscription**|C1RK1-WW5117-L731|Multi-year discounted subscription|
|**Monthly Subscription**|C1RK1-WW7891-L321|Month-to-month flexibility|
|**AutoCAD Flex**|001K1-00-8356|Pay-as-you-go tokens (Daily rate)|

### 4. Educational & Specialized SKUs

- **Education (EDU):** These do not typically have a commercial SKU for purchase but are identified by the suffix **-E001** in administrative portals.
    
- **Government (GOV):** SKUs for government entities often replace the "WW" (Worldwide) or "L" codes with specific contract identifiers (e.g., GSA schedules).
    

### How to Identify Your Specific SKU

If you are looking at an invoice or an Autodesk Account portal, the SKU follows a specific logic:

1. **First 5 Characters:** The Product Code (e.g., `C1R` for AutoCAD).
    
2. **Middle Segment:** The Region and Version Year.
    
3. **Final Segment:** The Term (e.g., `L1` for 1-year, `L3` for 3-year).
    

> **Note:** Because Autodesk frequently updates pricing and promotional codes, these "Base Pattern" SKUs may shift slightly by one or two digits depending on your specific reseller or if a "Promo SKU" is applied during checkout.
